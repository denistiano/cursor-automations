# Bulgaria AI coding competitor scan

**Date:** 2026-05-27  
**Analyst:** Cursor agent  
**Confidence:** medium-high  
**Scope:** Bulgarian businesses and Bulgaria-localized providers for AI coding, vibe coding, agentic coding, Cursor/Copilot training, and adjacent AI upskilling.

## Summary

The Bulgarian market already has direct competition for Vibe Coders Academy. SoftUni is the clearest direct competitor: it has a dedicated SoftUni AI brand, a Vibe Coding / AI-first programming pathway, developer courses for Copilot, Cursor, Claude Code, and an AI Agents and Workflows course covering n8n, LangChain, and LangGraph. NobleProg Bulgaria is the strongest corporate-training competitor for Copilot, Cursor, and agent workflows. Telerik Academy competes for professional developer upskilling with a polished GenAI for Developers sprint. IT STEP, Advance Academy, Aula.bg, and New Horizons are adjacent competitors that can absorb beginner, productivity, and corporate AI training demand.

## Strengths vs Vibe Coders Academy

- **SoftUni has breadth, brand, and SEO ownership.** It already covers the exact terms Vibe Coders Academy wants: AI-first programming, vibe coding, Cursor, Copilot, Claude Code, AI agents, n8n, LangChain, LangGraph, and production workflows.
- **SoftUni and Telerik have recognizable certificates.** This matters for students who value credential signaling.
- **NobleProg can sell to companies.** Its online/onsite format, tailored exercises, and Bulgaria locations make it credible for B2B training budgets.
- **Pricing anchors are already visible.** SoftUni's direct developer courses are low-cost per module, while NobleProg anchors premium corporate pricing.
- **Adjacent players capture non-developer AI demand.** Aula.bg and Advance Academy may own the beginner/productivity audience before those users consider coding.

## Gaps we can exploit

- **Cursor-first, project-first specialization.** SoftUni teaches many tools; Vibe Coders Academy can be narrower and deeper around Cursor Cloud Agents, rules, MCP, repo workflows, PRs, and production habits.
- **Senior practitioner / architecture angle.** Position around real engineering leadership, software architecture, enterprise delivery, testing, review, and operational discipline rather than generic AI productivity.
- **Small-cohort ownership and feedback.** Compete against large-scale platforms with hands-on review, direct feedback, and shipped artifacts.
- **Agentic business operations.** Blend coding agents with PM, Slack, automations, research, and launch operations; most competitors separate coding from operating a business/project with agents.
- **Bulgarian founder/operator positioning.** Build examples from local business use cases and practical constraints rather than generic demos.

## Market notes

- Searches for Bulgarian "vibe coding", "AI-assisted development", "Cursor AI", and "agentic coding" overwhelmingly returned SoftUni and NobleProg for direct developer offers.
- SoftUni AI publicly claims its SoftUni Vibe Coding program is the only Vibe Coding program for practical skills in Bulgaria; this claim should be treated as their marketing claim, not repeated as fact in our public copy without independent verification.
- I did not find a clearly branded independent Bulgarian "Cursor-first academy" competitor during this scan. The nearest direct substitutes are SoftUni's AI-Assisted Development and NobleProg's Cursor workflows course.

## Strategic recommendations

1. Position Vibe Coders Academy as **Cursor-first agentic software delivery**, not generic AI or beginner "no-code vibe coding".
2. Build the first offer around **one shipped project + repo workflow + PR/test/review discipline** to differentiate from broad lecture catalogs.
3. Track SoftUni and NobleProg weekly; they are the direct competitors most likely to shape pricing, keywords, and student expectations.
